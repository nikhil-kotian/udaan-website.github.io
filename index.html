Background

In 2018, nearly half of all rural fifth-grade students were unable to read a grade two text and two-thirds were unable to perform simple division*. Though we have achieved around 100% Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER)** in schools at every level and improved the percentage of schools with access to basic education infrastructure (84% national average)***, the quality of learning outcomes remains a significant concern, acknowledged in the newly passed National Education Policy (NEP).

*(“ASER Report 2018,” n.d.), **(“UDISE+ 2021-22”), ***(NITI Aayog SDG Index 2020-21)

Problem

Inadequate assessment process to identify learning gaps, linked to poor learning outcomes.

High Pupil-Teacher Ratio (PTR), negative impact on individualized attention in the classroom.

Language barriers in technology-based solutions for non-English speaking students

Solution

Technology-based system to strengthen assessment process and identify individual learning gaps.

Remedial solutions to fill the gaps and improve student performance.

Udaan tool to translate all the required content into regional languages
